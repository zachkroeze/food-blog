c is for:

cabbage
rolls

Ingredients

1 head

Savoy cabbage

1 kg

Turkey, ground

2 large

Eggs

1 medium

Onion, grated

1 medium

Onion, diced

3 cloves

Garlic

1/2 cup

Brown rice

2 tbsp

Butter

28 oz

Tomatoes, crushed

2 oz

Arugula

1/4 cup

Olive oil

5 tbsp

Lemon juice

1/2 cup

Brown sugar

1/2 cup

Golden raisins

1 handful

Parsley, chopped

4 dashes

Worchestershire sauce

Directions

Core the cabbage, and remove 12 leaves. Be patient during this process. Using a spoon to get under the leaves will help the process. Chop 1 cup of the remaining cabbage and set aside.

Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add leaves to boiling water in batches for 1 min. Set aside.

Preheat oven to 350F.

Over medium heat melt butter. Add diced onions, garlic, and chopped cabbage. Saute until onions are translucent and garlic is fragrant. Add crushed tomatoes, 3 tbsp of the lemon juice, brown sugar, worchestershire sauce, raisins, salt and pepper. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5 min. Take off heat and place enough of the sauce in the botton of a 13 x 9 in baking pan to cover the bottom.

Mix turkey, eggs, grated onion, rice, and add salt and pepper to taste. Divide mixture into 12 pieces and form to the shape of a football. Place the filling at the base of the cabbage leaves, fold in each side and roll to the top. Place in the sauced 13 x 9 in baking pan. Cover cabbage rolls with remaining sauce, cover with aluminium foil and bake for 2 hours.

Take cabbage rolls out of the oven and allow to rest for 5-10 min.

Combine remaining lemon juice with the olive oil. Pour over arugula and serve with the cabbage rolls.
